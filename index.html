What is Organizational Structure?

Organizational structure defines how people, roles, and responsibilities are organized within a business or institution. It outlines how information flows and how decisions are made across different levels of the organization.

Types of Organizational Structure

  • Hierarchical Structure: Traditional top-down structure.
  • Matrix Structure: Dual-reporting relationships.
  • Flat Structure: Few levels of management.
  • Divisional Structure: Based on product, region, or market.
  • Functional Structure: Divided based on job functions.

Why is it Important?

  • Clarifies roles and authority
  • Improves communication
  • Increases efficiency
  • Supports strategic objectives
